**Overview:** The RSS-25 engine, also known as the Space Shuttle Main Engine (SSME), is a high-performance liquid-fueled rocket engine used by NASA's Space Launch System (SLS) for deep space missions, like the Artemis program. The engine is able able to generate over 2 million pounds of thrust with liquid hydrogen and oxygen. Such an outcome is only possible through the implemented de Laval, or converging-diverging, rocket nozzles within the engine. The rocket nozzles are able to convert temperature and pressure generated by fuel combustion to thrust for propulsion.
